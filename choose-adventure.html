Project Overview

The outdoor recreation application industry lacked inclusivity, diversity, sustainability, and versatility, posing challenges for users from diverse backgrounds, including individuals with disabilities. This project involved conducting a comprehensive market analysis, including competitor research, target audience analysis, and user feedback, to refine the value proposition and business model for the future mobile application development of the ChooseAdventure brand. The goal was to address these shortcomings by gathering insights and making informed decisions that would enhance the app's value proposition, ensuring it catered to a wide range of outdoor enthusiasts and their diverse needs in a user-friendly and sustainable manner.



Market Analysis

The initial phase of the project involves conducting a thorough market analysis of the outdoor recreation application industry to gain a deeper understanding of the current market. This includes utilizing industry dynamics and competitive analysis to assess the competitive forces, intensity, and attractiveness of the outdoor recreation application industry. Additionally, a SWOT analysis is conducted to identify and evaluate the Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats of the outdoor recreation application industry. The market analysis provides a summary of the research findings and how they inform the design of the ChooseAdventure brand and mobile application. The goal is to create a solution that allows ChooseAdventure to differentiate itself from its competitors, by offering a unique and user-friendly application for outdoor recreation activities.



Industry Overview

The popularity of outdoor activities and technological advancements have led to a rapid growth of the outdoor recreation application industry. The COVID-19 pandemic has further fueled this growth as people seek safe and socially distant activities. Valued at USD 10.5 billion in 2019, the global market is projected to grow at a CAGR of 15.0% from 2020 to 2027. North America and Europe currently dominate the market, but Asia Pacific is expected to witness the highest growth rate due to increasing disposable incomes, growing interest in outdoor recreation, and adoption of technology.



Industry Dynamic

The competitive forces, intensity, and attractiveness of the outdoor recreation application industry can be summarized using the Porter Model framework as follows:

● Moderate to high threat of new entrants due to low barriers to entry.
● High threat of substitutes from alternative leisure activities and traditional methods of finding information about outdoor activities.
● Low supplier power due to the abundance of data sources and multiple providers of equipment and supplies.
● High buyer power due to a wide range of options, easily comparing prices and quality.
● Highly competitive, established brands have advantages over new companies.



Competitive Analysis



Competitive Analysis

AllTrails

AllTrails is a mobile and web-based application that provides users with information about hiking and biking trails, as well as other outdoor recreation activities such as skiing and snowshoeing.

  • Advantages

    • User-friendly and customizable interface, comprehensive database, detailed maps, interactive information exchange

  • Disadvantages

    • Premium subscription required for advanced features, unreliable user-generated content, US-focused trails, and high battery usage



Competitive Analysis

Mountain Project

Mountain Project is a website and mobile application that provides information on rock climbing areas, routes, and conditions around the world.

  • Advantages

    • Free user content, worldwide climbing database, community-driven, skill-level customization, user-friendly for beginners

  • Disadvantages

    • Focused on climbing, limited function, unreliable user content, no trail tracking, not useful for other outdoor recreation activities



Competitive Analysis

Slope

The Slope application is a mobile app designed for skiers and snowboarders to track their performance, navigate ski resorts, and connect with friends on the mountain.

  • Advantages

    • Interactive trail map with live recording, live location sharing and navigation, snow condition report, lift status, and weather forecast, in-depth analysis of skiing or snowboarding performance

  • Disadvantages

    • Focuses solely on ski and snowboard tracking, drains device battery, some advanced features only available with a premium subscription



Competitive Analysis

Gaia

The Gaia GPS is a mobile mapping software and application that provides a range of features for outdoor recreation, including hiking, hunting, fishing, camping, and offroading.

  • Advantages

    • Multi-device compatibility, highly detailed offline maps, comprehensive navigation, custom routes, simplified outdoor activity planning

  • Disadvantages

    • Expensive premium subscription, unintuitive interface, slow maps, GPS accuracy issues in remote and hazardous areas



SWOT Analysis




Problem Statement

The outdoor recreation application industry is facing challenges related to competitiveness, including a high threat of substitutes, low supplier power, and high buyer power. The lack of inclusivity, diversity, and sustainability in the outdoor recreation industry is not consistently prioritized in the design and development of applications and products. These challenges need to be addressed to ensure the industry's sustainable growth and to better serve users from diverse backgrounds, including those with disabilities, while promoting low-impact and sustainable outdoor recreation activities.



SWOT ANALYSIS

Opportunity

  • Leveraging local connections
  • Capitalizing on the DIY economy trend
  • Promoting a healthy lifestyle through outdoor recreation activities
  • Sustainable and low-impact outdoor recreation activities and products
  • Promoting diversity and inclusivity within the outdoor recreation industry
SWOT ANALYSIS

Threats

  • Climate change, seasonal nature, and natural disaster
  • High initial investment required for outdoor recreation equipment
  • Stringent regulations and permit requirements for certain activities
  • Liability concerns associated with injuries and accidents
  • Economic downturns can lead to decreased consumer spending on recreation activities

ChooseAdventure Brand Overview

ChooseAdventure is a company that is deeply committed to promoting sustainable and low-impact access to outdoor recreation activities and products. The company's mission is to make outdoor recreation activities accessible and inclusive to individuals of all backgrounds and experience levels while fostering diversity and inclusivity within the outdoor recreation industry.

ChooseAdventure is dedicated to promoting a healthy and active lifestyle through outdoor recreation activities that allow its users to connect with nature, enjoy its benefits, and achieve their fitness goals. The company believes in inspiring its users to develop a deep reverence and regard for nature and the environment. As part of this commitment, Choose Adventure encourages eco-cleanup activities and provides a platform for local leaders to create immersive outdoor experiences that showcase the beauty and diversity of the local environment to community members. The company also supports small local businesses and creates opportunities for individuals to connect with others.

ChooseAdventure recognizes the importance of the DIY economy trend and provides free education on gear selection, sizing, and maintenance, as well as basic first aid and wildlife education. The company's goal is to empower individuals to make informed decisions and take control of their outdoor experiences

Cost Structures & Revenue Streams

● Cost Structures

ChooseAdventure's primary development expenses involve hiring cross-functional teams for software development, database administration, project management, backend development, marketing, and UX design. Additional features like API integrations, e-commerce, phone sensors, geolocation, and IoT integrations can also increase development costs. Ongoing expenses such as server hosting, marketing, branding, and customer support influence maintenance costs. To reduce development costs, ChooseAdventure can collaborate with universities for internships or outsource development work rather than hiring an in-house team.

● Revenue Streans

ChooseAdventure can generate revenue through subscription, advertisement, donation, brokerage fees, and e-commerce. Premium subscription can unlock exclusive features such as advanced fitness tracking, offline navigation, maps, discounts on gears, and access to hosting outdoor recreation events. Ads from relevant businesses can be displayed within the app and through email campaigns. Donations can be encouraged from users who appreciate the app's mission. A fee can be charged for connecting users with third-party services. Lastly, the app can sell its own line of products to generate transaction-based revenue.



Customer Segment

Limitation and Future work

The outdoor recreation application industry faces significant challenges in terms of inclusivity, diversity, sustainability, and versatility. It has become apparent that the industry fails to adequately serve users from diverse backgrounds, including individuals with disabilities. Sustainability is often overlooked in the design and development of these applications, and the lack of versatile applications for various sports creates inconvenience for users who have to download multiple apps.

This project presents an opportunity to survey the outdoor recreation application industry, develop a business model canvas, and create a low-fidelity prototype for the ChooseAdventure application. The next step involves testing the low-fidelity prototype, gathering user feedback, and continuing development of the mid-fidelity prototype. This iterative process will culminate in the creation of the high-fidelity prototype, followed by user testing before final product development.

What I learned?

I have gained valuable insights into the integration of marketing and UX design strategies. I have learned how these two disciplines can work together synergistically to create successful products and experiences.

What I did well?

I have acquired a holistic approach to product development, where user-centered design principles are aligned with strategic marketing goals. This integrated approach has equipped me with valuable skills and knowledge to create products that not only meet user needs but also effectively communicate their value to the target market.

What could be improve?

I could have delved deeper into the specific concerns and requirements of users from diverse backgrounds, particularly those with disabilities. This would have allowed for a more comprehensive understanding of their needs and enabled the development of inclusive features within the application.
